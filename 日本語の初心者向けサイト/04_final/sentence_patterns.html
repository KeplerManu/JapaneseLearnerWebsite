Sentence Patterns

You can't simply form a sentence by putting two words together so in this section you'll learn how to formulate basic sentences with the use of some easy sentence structures, patterns, and particles

Politeness

In Japanese, there are levels of politeness similar to English. You wouldn't use the same language you use towards your friends as you would towards a higher up at work or a teacher. The following will teach you the sentence patterns in the polite form. You can use this towards strangers without sounding impolite.

List of Sentence Patterns

Below are a list of some basic structures / patterns / particles to help you create sentences:

  • Topic Marker and State of Being

    This is the most basic sentence structure in Japanese and is essentially used to say 'I am [x]', 'That is [x]', '[noun] is [x]'

    Structure:　Topic　は　Noun/Adjective　です。

    Explanation: The hiragana 「は」 (pronounced 'wa' when used as a topic marker) is used after a noun to mark it is the topic of a sentence. However, in Japanese, it is common to omit the topic/subject if the context is known. です (commonly pronounced as 'des', omitting the 'u' sound) is used to express the state of being of a noun, since there is verb for "to be" in japanese i.e. there is no 'am', 'is', etc.

    It is possible to turn your sentence into a question by adding the hiragana 「か」　after 「です」. Since 「か」 shows that the sentence is a question, it isn't necessary to add a question mark at the end of the sentence.

    Examples:　

    1. 私はだいがくせいです。　(Watashi wa daigakusei desu  /  I am a highschool student)

    2. A:  こんにちは。 元気ですか。　(Konnichiwa. Genki desu ka  /  Hello. Are you well?)
      Note: 「は」 in 「こんにちは」 is pronounced 'wa' because it was originally part of a larger sentence. This applies to 「こんばんは」 as well, which means good evening.

      B:  はい。 （私は）元気です。 (Hai. [Watashi wa] genki desu  /  Yes. [I am] well)
      Note: Since the context is known, it is possible to omit 「私は」 from this sentence.

    3. ねこはかわいいです。　(Neko wa kawaii desu  /  Cats are cute)

    Negative State of Being

    Structure: Noun/Adjective ではありません。

    Examples:　
    1. 私は日本人ではありません。　(Watashi wa nihonjin de wa arimasen  /  I am not Japanese)

    2. A: ジョンさんはだいがくせいですか。　(John-san wa daigakusei desu ka  /  Are you a university student, John?)
      Note: When talking to another person, Japanese people tend to use that person's name instead of 'you'. Also, adding the suffix 「さん」 onto the end of someone's name is the English equivalent of 'Mr. or Ms.' but is used much more frequently.

      B:  いいえ。 だいがくせいではありません。 まだこうこうせいです。 (iie. Daigakusei de wa arimasen. Mada koukousei desu.  /  No. [I'm] not a university student. [I'm] still a high school student.)

    3. いぬはかわいいではありません。　(Inu wa kawaii de wa arimasen  /  Dogs are not cute)

  • Inclusive particle

    Structure:　Noun1　も　Noun2　も　Noun3　です/ではありません

    Explanation: This particle has the same meaning as "as well as" or "also". This particle replaces 「は」.

    Examples:　
    1. A:　ケプラーさんもジョンさんもオーストラリア人ですか。　(Kepura-san mo Jon-san mo oosutorariajin desu ka  /  Are Kepler and John Australian?)

      B:　はい、ケプラーさんもジョンさんもオーストラリア人です。　(Hai, Kepura-san mo Jon-san mo oosutorariajin desu  /  Yes, Kepler and John are Australian)

  • Possessive/Ownership particle

    Structure:　Noun1　の　Noun2　です

    Explanation: This particle shows that Noun1 possesses/owns Noun2. Very easy to use and very straightfoward.

    Examples:　
    1. それは私のくるまです。　(Sore wa watashi no neko desu  /  That is my cat)

    2. それはきょうのしんぶんです。 (Sore wa kyou no shinbun  /  That is today's newspaper)

    3. これは私のいもうとのゲームです。　(Kore wa watashi no imouto no geemu desu  /  This is my sister's game)

  • Subject marker

    Structure:　(Noun1　は)　Noun2　が　adjective　です / います / あります

    Explanation: This particle is used mainly when trying to introduce or find out new information. A subject marker must always be used after a question word such as 「だれ」 who, 「どこ」 where, 「なに」 what, etc.　「います」 imasu / 「あります」 arimasu are the verbs for exists.

    Examples:　
    1. そのえいがはなにがおもしろいですか。　(Sono eiga ha nani ga omoshiroi desu ka  /  What is interesting about that movie?)

    2. 私はあにが一人います。 (Watashi wa ani ga hitori imasu  /  I have one older brother)

    3. どんなアニメがすきですか。　(Donna anime ga suki desu ka  /  What anime do [you] like?)
